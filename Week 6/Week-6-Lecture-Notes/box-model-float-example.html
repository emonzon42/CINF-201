Box Model

The Box Model is comprised of three main properties which are Padding, Border, and Margin. Content is also included but these three properties are what we will mainly use to affect the spacing of our content. Padding wraps around our content, border wraps around our padding, and finally, the margin wraps around all of it and serves as the buffer between content.

In the example below, I have added a 1px border to our paragraphs. The margin is still present but notice that vertical margins collapse into each other. Instead of there being 32px of margin between paragraphs, it is collapsed into 16px total.

Floats

Floats allow us to take content out of the normal flow of the page and place them on the left or right side of their containers. This enables text and inline elements to wrap around them. In the examples below, I wrap each set of floated items in a div with a class called "row". This allows me to "clear" our floats. When you don't clear floats, other elements on your page will try to take up available space and unintended behaviors may arise. I would recommend using the .row declarations (especially the before and after parts) for all class assignments moving forward. It will make it easier for you to separate content and keep the page behavior normal. For more info on clearing floats, read this article on floats by Chris Coyier.
